## Executive Summary

Sports betting affiliate marketing represents a multi-billion dollar market with CPA rates ranging from $100-$600+ per qualified customer acquisition. Baseball-specific betting tools remain underdeveloped compared to DFS analytics.

## 1. Affiliate Deal Economics

### CPA Rates (Cost Per Acquisition)

**Industry Standards (2024-2026 estimates):**
- **DraftKings:** $150-$300+ per qualified sign-up (varies by state and promotion)
- **FanDuel:** $150-$350+ per qualified sign-up
- **BetMGM:** $100-$250+ per qualified sign-up
- **Caesars:** $100-$200+ per qualified sign-up
- **Smaller books:** $50-$150 per sign-up

**Qualifications typically required:**
- User must register using affiliate link
- Must make initial deposit (usually $10-$50 minimum)
- Must place first bet (some programs)
- Must be in legal state with verified identity

**Revenue Share Alternative:**
- Some programs offer 20-35% revenue share on net gaming revenue (NGR)
- Hybrid models: upfront CPA + ongoing revenue share
- High-volume affiliates often negotiate custom deals

**State Variation:**
- Tier 1 states (NY, NJ, PA, IL, MI): highest CPAs ($200-$600)
- Tier 2 states (CO, VA, AZ, IN): moderate CPAs ($100-$250)
- New market launches: temporary bonuses ($300-$1,000+ for first customers)

### Key Finding
Web scraping attempts found that major sportsbooks (DraftKings, FanDuel) don't publicly list CPA rates - affiliates must apply to programs and negotiate based on traffic quality and volume.

## 11. Legal Requirements for Sports Betting Affiliates

### Licensing Requirements (by research attempt - exact regulations difficult to verify)

**Federal Level:**
- No federal affiliate license required
- Must comply with FTC disclosure requirements (affiliate relationships must be disclosed)
- Cannot target users in illegal jurisdictions

**State Level (varies significantly):**

**Strict Licensing States (require affiliate registration/license):**
- **New Jersey:** Affiliate vendors may need registration
- **Pennsylvania:** Affiliate marketing agreements must be disclosed to regulators
- **Illinois:** Potential licensing for affiliates in certain circumstances
- **Michigan:** May require service provider license for high-volume affiliates

**Moderate Oversight States:**
- Most states require sportsbooks to maintain responsible affiliate relationships
- Affiliates must not market to underage users
- Must include responsible gambling messaging

**Minimal Oversight States:**
- Many states have no specific affiliate licensing
- General advertising standards apply

### Key Compliance Requirements

**All Affiliates Must:**
1. Disclose affiliate relationships clearly
2. Include responsible gambling resources
3. Not target underage users (21+ in most states, 18+ in some)
4. Follow advertising standards set by state regulators
5. Geo-block users in illegal jurisdictions
6. Maintain records of affiliate agreements

**Tax Implications:**
- Affiliate income is taxable as business income
- May trigger nexus in states where you promote
- 1099 forms from affiliate networks

### Most Important States for Affiliates (by betting handle)

**Tier 1 (Essential):**
1. **New York** - largest legal betting market
2. **New Jersey** - mature market, high per-capita betting
3. **Pennsylvania** - large population, high handle
4. **Illinois** - major market, Chicago + suburbs
5. **Michigan** - strong betting participation

**Tier 2 (Valuable):**
6. Colorado
7. Virginia

**Tier 3 (Growing):**
- Maryland, Ohio, Louisiana, Massachusetts, Kansas, others

**Not legal:** California, Texas, Florida (large populations but not yet legal)

### Regulatory Bodies

## 13. Market Opportunities for Baseball Analytics + Betting

### Underdeveloped Niches

**1. Real-time Statcast Live Betting Tools**
- **Gap:** In-game odds don't reflect Statcast data
- **Opportunity:** Alert system for value bets based on pitch velocity drops, exit velocity trends
- **Monetization:** Subscription ($20-$50/month) + sportsbook affiliates

**2. Prop Prediction Models**
- **Gap:** Most prop content is expert opinion, not algorithmic
- **Opportunity:** ML models predicting strikeouts, hits, total bases using Statcast + weather + matchups
- **Monetization:** Freemium model (basic picks free, detailed analysis paid) + affiliates

**3. Umpire Analytics for Betting**
- **Gap:** Umpire tendencies undervalued in betting markets
- **Opportunity:** ABS challenge data reveals umpire accuracy/consistency
- **Monetization:** Premium subscription + sportsbook affiliates
- **Note:** This aligns with your ABS bot project

**4. Weather/Wind Tools for Totals Betting**
- **Gap:** Weather impacts often not priced correctly in real-time
- **Opportunity:** Automated alerts for wind shifts favoring over/under at outdoor parks
- **Monetization:** Subscription + affiliates

**5. Lineup Analysis for DFS + Betting**
- **Gap:** Ottoneu and real MLB lineup data can predict performance
- **Opportunity:** Your Ottoneu data could inform DFS and betting picks
- **Monetization:** Newsletter + Twitter + affiliates
