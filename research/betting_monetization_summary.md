## Legal Requirements (Summary)

### Federal
- FTC disclosure: clearly label affiliate links
- No targeting illegal jurisdictions
- Responsible gambling messaging

### State-Level
- Varies widely by state
- NJ, PA, IL may require affiliate registration
- Most states just require sportsbook to maintain responsible affiliate relationships
- Always include 21+ and problem gambling resources

### High-Value States (Priority Order)
1. New York - largest market
2. New Jersey - mature, high per-capita
3. Pennsylvania - large population
4. Illinois - Chicago metro
5. Michigan - strong participation

### Compliance Checklist
- [ ] Disclose all affiliate relationships
- [ ] Include responsible gambling links
- [ ] Age-gate content (21+ required)
- [ ] Geo-block illegal states if needed
- [ ] Maintain records of affiliate agreements
- [ ] Consult attorney in target states before launch

## Quick Decision Framework

### Should you pursue betting monetization?

**Yes, if:**
- You're comfortable with regulatory complexity
- You can commit to consistent content (3-5x/week minimum)
- Your day job allows side projects
- You're willing to build for 6-12 months before meaningful revenue
- You can handle platform risk (Twitter algorithm changes)

**No, if:**
- You want fast monetization (this is a 12-24 month build)
- You're uncomfortable with affiliate disclosure requirements
- You can't commit to content consistency
- You prefer pure analytics without betting angle

### Alternative: Stay Pure Analytics
**FanGraphs/Baseball Prospectus model:**
- Subscription-based ($5-$10/month)
- No betting controversy
- Lower revenue ceiling but cleaner brand
- Easier to maintain alongside day job

**Your projects work without betting:**
- ABS bot as pure baseball content
- Statcast visualizations for analysis
- Weather bot for fans, not bettors
- Ottoneu tools for league members
